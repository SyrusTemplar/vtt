1 A spell’s range is either the distance between the caster and the portion of the spell’s area of effect closest to the caster or between the caster and the center of the area of effect; the choice is the caster’s. Spells with touch range are always centered on the target the caster touches.

2 A spell with line-of-sight range can affect any target the caster can see, regardless of the range.

3 A spell with planar range can affect a target anywhere on the plane where the spell is cast. Use this category for any spell where the target’s location is not known.

4 A spell with trans-planar range actually reaches across planar boundaries. Only transport and reveal spells work at this range.
